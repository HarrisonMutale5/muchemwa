Iron Deficiency Aneamia

Aneamia is a heamatological disorder that is characterised by reduced Haemoglobin (Hb) levels in the blood. Aneamia is defined as a redaction in red blood cells Hb is a molecule that is attached to a red blood cell. Hb molecule consists of two tetramer proteins called heme and globin. the main function of Hb is to carry oxgyen to all parts of the body, so Aneamia develops if there is diminished Hb levels that can be due to :-

1) RBC's destraction eg. due to increased uric acid in renal problems such as kidney failure .
2) RBC's loss eg. via bleeding .
3) RBC's less production eg. cancer of the bone marrow.

Classification

1) Microcytic
2) Normocytic
3) Macrocytic
those three types are based on the size of the RBC's 
MCV = mean cell volume 

                    > MCV is used to measure the average 
                    volume of the red cell corpused
Normal ranges of MCV is 80 –100µm3
:therefore if MCV :
‐ less than 80µm3 that's Microcytic
‐ 80 - 100 µm3 Normalcytic
‐ greater than 100 Macrocytic (megaloblasts)

    Signs and Symptoms

    • Dizzness(ulushingwa, chizwezwe)
    • Palpitations (heart pamping very fast)
    • chest pains
    • craving for non nutritus foods (pica)
    • fatigue (feeling tired, Kulema kwa tupi)
    • Execrising intoleration
    • Shortness of breath
    • pallor (Appearing white in the palms and eyes due to shortage of blood)
    • Angular cheilitis(sores in the corners of the mouth) etc.

    Investigations

    1) full blood count
    2) haematocrit exemination
    3) Hb (this depends on sex of the patient)
    4) bone marrow exemination
    5) schilling test (this is used to determine how well can a patient Absorb vitamine B12 from thier intestinal tract)

    Treatment
